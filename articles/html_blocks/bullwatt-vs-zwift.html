Why choose BullWatt?

Choosing an open-source, web-based application like BullWatt over a heavy platform like Zwift comes down to speed, simplicity, and cost. While Zwift is known for its gamified virtual 3D worlds, BullWatt is specifically designed for cyclists who want a frictionless, "no-nonsense" structured workout.

Here are the primary advantages of choosing BullWatt:
  • Completely Free: Unlike Zwift, which requires a paid subscription, BullWatt is a 100% free indoor cycling app. There are no monthly fees, no credit cards required, and no limits tied to free trials.
  • Zero Downloads or Installations: Apps like Zwift and MyWhoosh often require massive downloads (sometimes 10GB+) and high-end graphics cards. BullWatt bypasses this entirely by running natively in your Google Chrome browser using Web Bluetooth. You can start a session instantly on a PC, Mac, or Android device without downloading any software.
  • No Loading Screens or Registration: The creator built BullWatt directly to solve the frustration of long loading times on other apps. It features a minimalist design with no loading times, no ads, and no user registration required. It is the fastest way to get on your bike and start training.
  • Pure Focus on Power-Regulated Training: Instead of navigating a virtual world, BullWatt focuses strictly on precise power control using ERG mode. It automatically adjusts your smart trainer's resistance based on your cadence and your personal Functional Threshold Power (FTP) to keep you in the correct wattage zones. It also includes a robust catalog of pre-set sessions, from Sweet Spot Training (SST) to VO2 Max intervals.
  • Built for Multitasking: Because it is a lightweight web app, BullWatt is ideal for riders who prefer their own entertainment. You can easily use split-screen mode to watch Netflix or YouTube while you train. The app includes a "Wake Lock" feature to ensure your screen doesn't go to sleep in the middle of a hard interval.
  • Hardware Simplicity: You do not need an ANT+ dongle or extra hardware to use the app. As long as you have Bluetooth enabled on your device and use Chrome, it connects directly to standard FTMS-compatible smart trainers.
  • Data Privacy and Open-Source Transparency: BullWatt does not rely on server-side backups; your recent sessions are simply stored locally within your browser. You can easily export your workouts as TCX files or auto-sync them to Strava to track your fitness alongside outdoor rides. Furthermore, its codebase is hosted on a GitHub repository, reflecting its open-source and community-accessible nature.

Ultimately, if you want to avoid expensive overhead and heavy 3D graphics in favor of instant, high-quality, power-regulated workouts, BullWatt provides a streamlined alternative to Zwift.
