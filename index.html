I was bored by long loading times in other apps (like Zwift, MyWhoosh, Rouvy...). My goal was to create a fast way to launch an activity and record it. For me, a good session means working on wattage zones, which is why wattage control is the only option.

Click on Start, then click on Connect. You can adjust your power with buttons or key arrows (up, down, left, right). That's it! Don't forget to click on Stop to store the activity.

Bluetooth FTMS (Fitness Machine Service) is a Bluetooth profile that enables standardized communication between fitness equipment and apps for data exchange and control.

Functional Threshold Power (FTP) is the highest average power a cyclist can sustain for approximately one hour, measured in watts. It's a key metric used to gauge a rider's fitness, track progress, and establish personalized training zones.

You can find all your sessions stored in TCX format. The last sessions are registered in your browser; there is no server-side backup/save.

We use the same mechanism as YouTube to prevent sleeping. You need to keep the training tab visible. You can use the split-screen feature to watch YouTube or Netflix while training.

Yes! BullWatt is a 100% free indoor cycling app. Unlike Zwift or TrainerRoad, there are no monthly fees, no "free trial" limits, and no credit card required. It is designed for cyclists who want to train on their home trainer without the overhead of a expensive subscription.

BullWatt runs directly in your Google Chrome browser using Web Bluetooth. This means you don't need to download or install any software. Simply open the website, connect your FTMS-compatible trainer, and start your ERG mode workout instantly on any PC, Mac, or Android device.

Absolutely. As long as you use Google Chrome, BullWatt works on Android phones, tablets, and laptops. It leverages the Web Bluetooth API to talk directly to your bike, making it one of the most lightweight mobile training solutions available.

BullWatt supports any smart trainer that uses the standard Bluetooth FTMS protocol. This includes popular models like the Wahoo KICKR, Tacx Neo/Flow, Elite Suito/Direto, and Decathlon Domyos bikes. If your trainer supports "Controllable" Bluetooth, it will work with BullWatt.

BullWatt is specifically designed for structured training. It offers a catalog of pre-set sessions—from SST (Sweet Spot Training) to VO2 Max intervals—all controlled via ERG mode. It's the ideal tool for riders who want a "no-nonsense" workout player that loads in seconds.

On the BullWatt home page, you can easily input your FTP (Functional Threshold Power) in the "Setup Your FTP" box. All the catalog workouts will then automatically scale the resistance to your specific fitness level, ensuring you are training in the correct power zones.
